Close-loop Brain-Heart Machine Interface

MEng design project in a group of 3 students with 2 adviors(one from ECE, another from the Human Ecology)

Project Objective

Inspired by the Brain-Computer Interface, our outside advisor Yu Hao wanted to explore the "Heart-Computer Interface" since the heart beats is relatively easier to collect than the Brain related signals like EEG or fMRI. So she initiated this project to develop a system that can be used in further experiments. However, there was nothing but an idea of "Heart-Computer Interface". We explored what the system was going to achieve and how it should function together in the process. She advised us on biological and psychological knowledge while made requirement about how the system function and what kind of effect she wanted at the end. In a way, she acted like a customer and we need to design and implement to make her happy. For us, this project was comprehensive in which we needed to perform different roles like product manager, system designer, software/hardware developer, tester and recorder while kept good communication with the customer.

Project Introduction

The project can be devided to four stages. During the first stage, we decided the devices we were about to use like sensor and microcontroller, and initiated the design. Then the work was assigned to different people for implementation. For the second stage, we implemented some experiements and summrized what we need to changed or added. Then made modification of the design according to the new request of the outside advior. In the third stage, we implemented the modified plan and finalized the implementation. At last, we conducted more experiments, solved some performance issues and summerized our work.
Poster Report

My Role and Team Collaberation

There were three students in this project including me, Hao Wu and Naiqing Zhou. Initially since I had experience on the microcontroller and related hardware system in college, I was responsible of designing and building the system. Hao investigated how to use Pan Tompkins Algorithm to detect heart rate peak. Then Naiqing mainly searched research work we need and documented what we did. After we modified the design, it was mainly me and Hao Wu worked together to implemented the interface in the Matlab and solved the performance issues. So it was Naiqing who put together the report and the poster at last based on the details we offered. We three normally did the experiements together.
My work covered both hardware and software. The part related to microcontroller was done by myself, as well as the communication bettween the microcontroller and Matlab interface. Half part of the matlab interface was implemented by me. Hao Wu and I worked together to solve the performance issue which signels from two channel interfere with each other. Since Hao Wu and I were more familiar with technical details and Hao was a little shy about talking in the meeting, I normally presented our work for the team in our weekly meeting with ECE side of adivor Bruce Land.
