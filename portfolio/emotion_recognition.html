What's matter?

Milestone for the machine becoming more intelligent

I won't write the significance of emotion recognition like what the introduction section always states in the paper. I will talk about why this topic attracts me. In BCMI lab, there are a couple of meaningful on-going research topics including fatigue detection, emotion recognition, mental disorder detection(onto depression recently). I chose to go deep in emotion recognition because it enables the machine understand the feeling of human which can be the foundation of affectiving computer and other applications like detecting mental disorders. This aligns with my hope of achieving truely intelligent system. However, emotion is so complicated that single factor can not accurately interpret it. So, I like the idea of multimodal which combine different modalities for complementary information like what human would do. We would never only trust what we see or what we hear. We are smart enough to look at the big picture and consider all the factors. I think the machine should do that as well -- smart enough to look at the whole picture. Besdies different modalities, diversity among subjects shoulde also be considered in that picture. How to find the common space among unique subjects also matters. That's why I'd love to invesitigate transfer learning with multimodal architecture.

First Outcome

Combining different domains of EEG feature for emotion recognition

EEG signal contains various infomation from different aspects. For time domain, EEG signals are time series where previous emotional states may influence later ones. For frequency domain, EEG signal can be filtered by five frequency bands and each band has their importance on recognizing differernt emotions. From the perspective of spatial domain, there exists critical connection between different brain areas or different electrodes for emotion recognition. In this manner, combining time series, frequency features and topological structure of EEG signal can fully utilize its information for emotion recognition which will lead to better performance.
I think the greatest value of this study is that EEG signal is relatively hard to collect in the real world, whereas it is also very effective for emotion recogntion. So it is worthwhile investigating how to fully take advantages of it. For me, this study is a great opportunity to learn EEG signals and emotion recognition at the beginning. I read many related work to fully understand it. It was my honor to summerize it and put it together as a paper with other researchers. This is also the first paper that I wrote which accelerates my pace of study. It was existed to see it published on MM '21: Proceedings of the 29th ACM International Conference on Multimedia.

Second Outcome

Multimodal with Transformer Architecture

I took part in the experiments of collecting anger and surprise emotion states. Among six basic emotion (happiness, sadness, disgust, fear, anger and surprise), there exists datasets of other four types of emotions with EEG and eye movement signals (SEED series datasets). EEG and eye movement signals on anger and surprise remain unknown. So it is worthwhile conducting such experiements and invesitigating the discriminative and complimentary properties of EEG and eye movement signals on recognizing anger and surprise. I conducted around 30 experiements for 10 subjects with three sessions each.
Besides conducting experiments, I investigated the complimentary properties of EEG and eye movement signals on recognizing anger and surprise while another researcher worked on discriminative ability. My work was kind of based on his study since we need to prove the discriminative ability before going deeper for complimentary properties. While I was dealing with multimodal structures, I started to see the value of utilizing different modalities by reading many other studies. This is also a reason that why I still focus on multimodal structure now. During the study, Transformer architecture attracted me since it achieves great improvement on CV and NLP tasks with better parallelization. Besides, I think the idea of pure attention mechanism fits emotion related tasks because that's how human emotion works. So I wanted to utilize Transformer structure with multimodal on studying complimentary properties of EEG and eye movement signals on recognizing anger and surprise. This work was done by myself including summarizing the work and publishing the paper on IEEE BIBM'21. I will put on the resource after the conference of BIBM '21 is held.
